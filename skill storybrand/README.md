## 🎯 The 7-Part Framework at a Glance

| Part | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1. Character | Your customer is the hero | Centers patient needs, not clinic features |
| 2. Problem | External + internal + philosophical obstacles | Creates relatability and emotional connection |
| 3. Guide | You demonstrate empathy + authority | Builds trust without being arrogant |
| 4. Plan | Simple 3-step process | Removes confusion and makes "yes" easy |
| 5. Action | Clear, repeated CTAs | Tells customers exactly what to do |
| 6. Success | Character transformation | Paints the desired future outcome |
| 7. Failure | Consequences of not acting | Creates urgency without fear-mongering |

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Tip 1: Customer-Centric Language
❌ "We specialize in smile design with 20 years of experience"  
✅ "You deserve a smile that matches your confidence. We know how to create it."

### Tip 2: Problem Specificity
❌ "For people with teeth problems"  
✅ "If you feel self-conscious about your smile in photos and professional meetings"

### Tip 3: Simple Plans
Don't overwhelm with steps. Three is perfect:
1. [Assessment/Listening]
2. [Solution/Creation]
3. [Transformation/Results]

### Tip 4: Repeat Your CTA
Mention it at least 3–5 times across any page or content piece. Different wording each time keeps it fresh.

### Tip 5: Make Stakes Clear
Patients need to feel urgency. What happens if they don't act?
- Health deteriorates
- Confidence wanes
- Opportunities are missed
- Costs become higher later

### Tip 6: Paint the Transformation
Don't just say "happy." Describe the experience:
- "You catch your reflection unexpectedly and smile without thinking"
- "Friends notice immediately: 'Your smile looks amazing!'"
- "You're finally in photos, not avoiding them"

## ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Talking about yourself instead of the customer**
   - ❌ "We have the latest technology"
   - ✅ "You'll experience cutting-edge precision"

2. **Skipping the problem (Part 2)**
   - Customers buy solutions to problems, not products
   - Make sure the problem is vivid and relatable

3. **Weak guide section (Part 3)**
   - Empathy alone = patronizing
   - Authority alone = arrogant
   - Both together = credible

4. **Complicated plans (Part 4)**
   - If patients can't remember your 5-step process, it's too long
   - Three steps, maximum

5. **Assuming one CTA is enough (Part 5)**
   - Busy patients miss it
   - Repeat your primary ask throughout
   - Offer secondary CTAs for less-committed visitors

6. **Generic success descriptions (Part 6)**
   - "Beautiful smile" doesn't inspire action
   - Specific transformation does: "From hiding in photos to leading family picture day"

7. **Forgetting the stakes (Part 7)**
   - No consequences = no urgency
   - Make clear what happens if they postpone
