# StoryBrand Application Guide – Content Types

This guide shows how to apply the 7-part framework to different content pieces and marketing channels.

---

## Landing Page (Uses All 7 Parts)

**Structure**:
- Hero section: Parts 1 + 2 (Hero's desire + Problem)
- Value proposition: Part 3 (Guide + Empathy)
- How it works: Part 4 (The Plan)
- CTA section: Part 5 (Call to Action)
- Results showcase: Part 6 (Success/Transformation)
- Fear/stakes section: Part 7 (Failure/Consequences)

**Key Copywriting**:
- **H1 (Hero)**: Speak directly to the customer's desire. "Finally, a Smile That Matches Your Confidence"
- **Subheadline (Problem)**: Name the specific problem they face. "If you've been hiding your smile, this is for you."
- **Value Prop (Guide)**: Show empathy + authority. "We've helped 500+ patients in Temuco transform not just their smile, but their confidence."
- **Plan section**: "Here's how it works: 1) We listen, 2) We design, 3) You transform."
- **Primary CTA**: "Schedule Your Transformation Consultation"
- **Social proof**: Before/after + testimonials = success (Part 6)
- **Closing**: "Without addressing this now, your smile will continue to age, and opportunities for confidence will pass by."

---

## Blog Post (Parts 2, 3, 4, 6)

Focus on the patient's problem (Part 2), your expertise (Part 3), and the transformation possible (Part 6).

**Structure**:
1. **Opening**: Acknowledge the problem deeply (empathy)
2. **Body**: Educate on the solution (authority + plan)
3. **Closing**: Paint the transformation (success) + CTA

**Example Outline: "Why Your Smile Isn't Just Cosmetic—It's Confidence"**
- Opening: "If you've felt self-conscious about your smile, you're not alone. [Internal problem]"
- Section 1: "The Real Cost of Delaying Smile Transformation" (Problem = Part 2)
- Section 2: "Why San Martín Dent Approaches Smile Design Differently" (Guide = Part 3)
- Section 3: "Your 3-Step Smile Transformation Journey" (Plan = Part 4)
- Closing: "From hiding to shining—that's the transformation our patients experience. Ready? [CTA]"

**SEO Benefit**: Blog posts answering "What is a smile makeover?" or "How does smile design work?" naturally speak to Parts 2–4, building trust before the sales conversation.

## Social Media Post (Parts 1, 2, 6)

Short-form: Focus on relatability (hero), problem acknowledgment, and transformation.

**Instagram/TikTok Formula**:
- **Opening (hook)**: Name the hero's problem in 3 seconds. "If you've felt embarrassed about your smile..."
- **Body (pattern interrupt)**: Show contrast. "But what if that could change?"
- **Close (transformation)**: Show the before/after or desired state. "This is what confidence looks like." + CTA link in bio

**Example Instagram Caption**:

"Hiding your smile in photos? You don't have to. 😊

Many of our patients came in feeling self-conscious about their teeth. But after their smile transformation, they're smiling freely—in family photos, professional meetings, everywhere.

[Before/After image]

Your smile is waiting for you. Let's create it together.

Link in bio → Schedule your consultation"

**Key Elements**:
- Hero acknowledgment: "If you..."
- Problem validation: "Many feel..."
- Transformation vision: "After..."
- Direct CTA: "Schedule your consultation"

## Email Sequence (Parts 1–7, Spread Across Multiple Messages)

**Email 1 – Hero & Problem (Awareness)**
- Subject: "The one thing holding back your confidence"
- Body: Acknowledge the hero and their specific problem (what they're searching for)
- CTA: Transitional ("Download: The Smile Transformation Guide")

**Email 2 – Guide (Credibility)**
- Subject: "Why we approach smile design differently"
- Body: Demonstrate empathy + authority; tell a patient success story
- CTA: Transitional ("Watch: Real Transformation Stories")

**Email 3 – Plan (Clarity)**
- Subject: "Here's exactly how we create your smile"
- Body: Explain the 3-step process simply
- CTA: Direct ("Schedule Your Consultation")

**Email 4 – Success & Failure (Motivation)**
- Subject: "What you're going to experience"
- Body: Paint vivid picture of transformation (Part 6) + remind of what they'll miss (Part 7)
- CTA: Direct ("Book Your Transformation Today")

## Sales Conversation (All 7 Parts, Live)

You don't read a script, but you *follow the structure*.

**Opening (Parts 1–2)**
- "Tell me, what brought you in today?" Listen for their hero's desire and problem
- Reflect back: "So you're looking to feel more confident in your smile, and right now [problem] is in the way."
- Show empathy: "That's something I hear from almost every patient who walks through that door."

**Middle (Parts 3–4)**
- "Here's how we're different..." (Authority)
- "And here's exactly how we'd approach your transformation..." (Plan)
- Draw a simple diagram: Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3

**Close (Parts 5–7)**
- "Here's what I'd recommend we do..." (Direct CTA)
- "If we don't start now, your smile will continue aging, and you'll keep having these moments of self-consciousness. But if we do this, in [timeframe], you're going to walk past a mirror and smile without thinking—because you'll love what you see." (Success + Failure)

## Before & After Post (Parts 6 + Implicit 2)

**Caption Structure**:
- **Hero**: "Meet [First Name] (anonymized)" or "One of our favorite transformations"
- **Before state (Problem)**: "She came in feeling self-conscious about her smile"
- **The journey (Plan)**: "Over [timeframe], we..."
- **After state (Success)**: "Now she's smiling freely in every moment"
- **CTA**: "This could be your story. Schedule your consultation."

**Important**: Always get explicit patient consent. Use real photos, never AI-generated. Frame as a transformation story, not a before-and-after ad.

## Educational Video Script (Parts 2, 3, 4)

**Structure** (3–5 minute video):

**Intro (0:00–0:15)**
- Hook the problem: "If you've wondered how smile design actually works, watch this."

**Problem Deep-Dive (0:15–1:30)**
- Name the external problem (misaligned teeth, discoloration, worn teeth)
- Name the internal problem (self-consciousness, avoidance)
- Show examples or clips

**Authority + Plan (1:30–4:00)**
- "Here's how we approach it differently..."
- Show the 3 steps visually or via screen recording
- Include testimonial or example patient journey (anonymized)

**Call to Action (4:00–4:30)**
- "Ready to see if smile design is right for you? [CTA]"
- Link in description

**Example Hook**: "Most people think smile design is just about whitening. But it's so much more. Here's what actually goes into creating your perfect smile."

## Funnel Architecture (How Stages Map to the Framework)

**Cold Traffic (First Exposure)**
- Focus: Parts 1 + 2
- Goal: Grab attention by naming their problem
- Format: Social ads, blog headlines, video hooks
- Message: "Are you hiding your smile?" or "Feeling self-conscious about your teeth?"

**Warm Traffic (Early Nurture)**
- Focus: Parts 3 + 4
- Goal: Build trust and show your process
- Format: Blog posts, email sequences, educational content, testimonials
- Message: "Here's how we approach smile transformation differently"

**Hot Traffic (Ready to Buy)**
- Focus: Parts 5, 6, 7
- Goal: Clarify the CTA, paint success, remind of stakes
- Format: Landing pages, sales conversations, final email sequence
- Message: "Schedule now and transform your confidence—here's what to expect"

## Pro Tips

1. **Different audiences, same framework**: Cold traffic emphasizes Parts 1–2. Warm traffic emphasizes Parts 3–4. Hot traffic emphasizes Parts 5–7.

2. **Repeat your CTA differently**: In blog posts, CTA is conversational ("Ready? Schedule a consultation"). On social, it's direct ("Link in bio"). On landing pages, it's prominent and repeated.

3. **Let authenticity guide tone**: For TikTok/Reels, be casual and conversational. For white papers or blog posts, be authoritative but accessible. For sales conversations, be consultative and listening-focused.

4. **Patient stories are gold**: Any real patient journey that follows the 7-part arc (problem → guide → plan → transformation) becomes your most powerful marketing. Collect these stories systematically.

5. **Test and iterate**: Track which version of each part resonates most. "Are you hiding your smile?" vs. "Self-conscious about your smile?" will have different conversion rates. A/B test and measure.

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Reverse the framework**: Talking about yourself instead of the customer  
❌ **Skipping Part 5**: Being unclear about what you want them to do  
❌ **Weak Part 3**: Forgetting to demonstrate authority or empathy  
❌ **Generic Part 6**: Saying "beautiful smile" instead of painting the transformation  
❌ **Ignoring Part 7**: Forgetting that stakes create urgency  

---

Use this guide to ensure every piece of marketing content you create follows the StoryBrand structure, whether it's a 10-second social post or a 5-minute video.
